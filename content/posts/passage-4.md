## Content

There has been a debate on whether we should study in groups or study alone to be more effective. While self-learning do brings lots of benefits, I believe that group study is a better method to acquire new knowledges.

On the one hand, self-learning can be very effective when you are deep-diving into a specific area of knowledge, where only a few people have heard about. It would be quite insufficient to discuss some uncommon topics with someone who can't really relate themselves to. Besides, self-learning is essential when someone needs to concentrate. When someone is preparing for an exam, it is better not to find some revise partner since every exam point is in the textbook.

But one person's knowledge base is limited because it is determined by the physical limitation of our brain, and that's where group study comes in. Group study is an efficient means to complement our knowledge and ideas together, forming a bigger database that can benefit every member of the group. Through the process of communicating with each other, we can gradually form lots of beneficial traits. For instance, we can foster critical thinking while debating with different opinions, along with effective communication skills. Like Confucious once said: "Among three people walking together, there will always be one who can be my teacher", which is a perfect summary of the benefits of group studying.

To summarize, I believe that studying alone can be useful and effective at some times, but eventually we need to study together to gain more knowledges and make progress together.
