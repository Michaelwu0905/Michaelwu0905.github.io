The diagrams compare the existing ground floor layout of a house with a proposed plan for future building work.

Overall, the proposed changes would make the first floor more open by removing the hall and some internal walls. The kitchen and living room would stay in the same general positions, but the kitchen would add more furniture, while the stairs and doors would be rearranged.

In the existing plan, the kitchen is located in the left side of the house, which is seperated from the hall by an internal wall. There is an entrance door at the top left of the plan. In the proposed layout, the kitchen would remain in the same space, but more kitchen furniture would be installed along the kitchen wall. Additionally, two internal doors would be placed between the kitchen and the central area, while replacing the single doorway of the current design.

The central part of the house would change more significantly in the proposed plan. Right now, there is a hall between the kitchen and the living room. There are also stairs and storage area uder the stairs. In the new plan, the hall and the storage area would be removed to create a larger open area around the stairs. The internal wall between the hall and the living room would also be taken away, in order to let the living room connect with the central space directly. However, the living room would remain on the right side of the house, while its external shape would stay unchanged.
