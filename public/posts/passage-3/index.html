IELTS writing#

Some people argue that the money spent by governments on space programmes would be better spent on vital public services such as schools and hospitals. I partly agree with this statement. I believe that space programmes also brings long-term benefits to society and should not be cut completely.

On the one hand, it is true that those vital public services are the backbone of our society and it should be the top agenda for every government. Schools provide knowledge and skills for children and teenagers, while hospitals have the duty to protect citizen’s health and save lives. If these public services are underfunded, the whole society would suffer. Therefore, when a government’s budget is limited, these vital public services should be the top priority to consider.

On the other hand, this does not mean that those space programmes are waste of money. Space missions can bring strategical long-term benefits. For instance, satellites orbiting the earth are used for weather forecasting, global communication, disaster monitoring, etc. These are essential part of our daily life that bring us convenience and safety. These satellites are useful in our daily life and can also help governments to deal with serious natural issues such as storms, floods and climate change. Additionally, space projects encourage young people to devote themselves into technology, engineering and science, which can benefit a country’s future development.

To summarize, I partly agree with the view that schools and hospitals should get more financial supports from the government, especially when they are lack of basic funds. However, that doesn’t mean space programmes are all useless and should be completely abandoned, since space projects can still bring actual scientific, practical and educational benefits.
